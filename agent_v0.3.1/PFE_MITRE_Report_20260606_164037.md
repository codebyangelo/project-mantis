## 6. Executive Incident Synthesis
**Who Attributed:** Iaman Informant (Internal Employee)

**What Happened:** The suspect, an employee of the technology development division, exfiltrated sensitive company intellectual property, including project design concepts, pricing decisions, and proposals, as part of a planned data leakage to a rival company conspirator.

**Where Transferred:** Personal cloud storage and physical storage devices (USB memory stick and CD).

**How Stolen:** The suspect utilized their authorized access to bypass internal DRM/DLP controls, copying sensitive files to unauthorized external media and personal cloud storage, and attempted to remove these devices from the premises.

**When Occurred:** March 23, 2015, to March 25, 2015.

**Incident Narrative:**
The incident involved an internal threat actor, 'Iaman Informant,' who conspired with an employee from a rival firm to exfiltrate proprietary technology data. Between March 23 and March 25, 2015, the suspect systematically accessed and staged sensitive project files, including design concepts and pricing decisions, as evidenced by the creation of LNK shortcut files in the user's Recent items directory. The suspect communicated with the conspirator via email to coordinate the transfer of these materials.

Following the staging of data, the suspect attempted to exfiltrate the remaining large volumes of information using physical storage media, specifically a USB memory stick and a CD. The activity was detected at the company's security checkpoint on March 25, 2015. While the initial physical inspection of the devices did not immediately reveal the stolen data, the suspect was flagged for suspicious behavior, leading to the seizure of the devices and the workstation for forensic analysis.

## 7. NIST SP 800-61r2 Alignment & Containment Recommendations
**Incident Lifecycle Phase:** Detection & Analysis -> Transitioning to Containment, Eradication, & Recovery

### Immediate Containment Actions
* Immediate revocation of the suspect's network and physical access credentials.
* Isolation of the suspect's workstation from the corporate network to prevent further data synchronization.
* Seizure and forensic imaging of the suspect's physical storage devices (USB and CD) and workstation.

### Eradication & Recovery
* Perform a full forensic wipe and re-image of the compromised workstation.
* Purge all unauthorized copies of sensitive project data from personal cloud storage accounts identified during the investigation.
* Conduct a comprehensive audit of all access logs to ensure no other backdoors or unauthorized access points were established.

### Post-Incident Activity
* Enhance DLP (Data Loss Prevention) and DRM (Digital Rights Management) policies to restrict the movement of sensitive files even for privileged users.
* Implement stricter physical security controls at checkpoints, including automated scanning for unauthorized storage media.
* Integrate threat intelligence to monitor for communications with known rival entities and implement behavioral analytics to detect anomalous file access patterns.
