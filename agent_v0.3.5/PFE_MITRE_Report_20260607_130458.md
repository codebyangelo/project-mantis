## 6. Executive Incident Synthesis
**Who Attributed:** Iaman Informant (Internal Employee/Manager)

**What Happened:** The suspect, a manager at the company, engaged in unauthorized data exfiltration of sensitive 'Secret Project' intellectual property. Stolen data included pricing decisions, design concepts, and project proposals, which were staged on local storage and transferred to external removable media (USB) and a remote network location.

**Where Transferred:** External removable USB drive (E: drive) and a remote network share (10.11.11.128).

**How Stolen:** The suspect utilized LNK files to access and stage sensitive documents from internal directories to removable media and a remote IP address. The activity involved bypassing internal DLP/DRM controls using authorized managerial privileges.

**When Occurred:** 2015-03-23 to 2015-03-25

**Incident Narrative:**
Between March 23 and March 25, 2015, the suspect, identified as 'Iaman Informant,' executed a planned data exfiltration scheme. On March 23, the suspect accessed sensitive project files, including design concepts and proposals, and exfiltrated them to an unauthorized USB device (E: drive). Simultaneously, the suspect leaked pricing decision documentation to a remote network share at 10.11.11.128. Forensic analysis of LNK artifacts confirms these actions were deliberate and targeted at 'Secret Project' data. By March 24, the suspect began staging further documentation, including a resignation letter, on the local desktop in preparation for departure. The activity was ultimately identified following the suspect's interception at a company security checkpoint while attempting to remove storage devices from the premises.

## 7. NIST SP 800-61r2 Alignment & Containment Recommendations
**Incident Lifecycle Phase:** Detection & Analysis -> Transitioning to Containment, Eradication, & Recovery

### Immediate Containment Actions
* Immediate revocation of the 'informant' user account credentials.
* Network isolation of the suspect's workstation to prevent further unauthorized outbound connections.
* Disable all USB mass storage device mounting capabilities via Group Policy Object (GPO) across the environment.
* Block outbound traffic to the identified malicious IP address 10.11.11.128 at the perimeter firewall.

### Eradication & Recovery
* Perform a full forensic wipe and re-image of the suspect's workstation.
* Conduct a comprehensive audit of all network shares accessed by the 'informant' account to identify additional compromised data.
* Purge all malicious LNK artifacts and staged files from the user's profile and temporary directories.

### Post-Incident Activity
* Enhance DLP monitoring to alert on file access patterns involving sensitive project directories by privileged users.
* Implement stricter controls on the use of removable media, enforcing hardware-level encryption and logging for all authorized devices.
* Integrate endpoint detection and response (EDR) telemetry to flag the creation of LNK files pointing to external or non-standard network paths.
* Conduct a review of access control lists (ACLs) for sensitive project data to ensure the principle of least privilege is strictly enforced.
