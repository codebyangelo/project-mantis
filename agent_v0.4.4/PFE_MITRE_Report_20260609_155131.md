## 6. Executive Incident Synthesis
**Who Attributed:** Mr. Informant (an internal employee/manager of the technology development division)

**What Happened:** The suspect exfiltrated sensitive corporate intellectual property, specifically files related to a 'Secret Project', including a design concept presentation and a project proposal, by copying them to an unauthorized external USB storage device.

**Where Transferred:** An unauthorized external USB storage device (identified as E:\ drive, labeled 'Authorized USB' to mask its illicit nature).

**How Stolen:** The suspect utilized Windows LNK files to facilitate and track access to sensitive files stored on the unauthorized removable media. The exfiltration was performed by manually copying files from the corporate network/local environment to the E:\ drive, bypassing internal DLP/DRM controls due to the suspect's elevated managerial privileges.

**When Occurred:** 2015-03-23 between 18:37:54 UTC and 18:38:23 UTC.

**Incident Narrative:**
On March 23, 2015, the suspect, identified as 'Mr. Informant', engaged in the unauthorized exfiltration of proprietary 'Secret Project' data. Forensic analysis of the user's Recent Items folder revealed the creation of malicious LNK files that pointed directly to an external E:\ drive, which the suspect had labeled 'Authorized USB' to evade detection. These LNK files, specifically '[secret_project]_proposal.LNK' and '[secret_project]_design_concept.LNK', confirm that the suspect accessed and moved sensitive project documentation to the removable media during the evening hours. This activity constitutes a direct violation of the company's security policy, which strictly forbids the use of unauthorized portable storage devices for handling confidential electronic files. The suspect's actions were part of a broader, premeditated plan to leak state-of-the-art technology secrets to a rival company for financial gain. The incident was eventually detected when the suspect attempted to pass through the company's security checkpoint with the storage devices.

## 7. NIST SP 800-61r2 Alignment & Containment Recommendations
**Incident Lifecycle Phase:** Detection & Analysis -> Transitioning to Containment, Eradication, & Recovery

### Immediate Containment Actions
* Immediately revoke the suspect's network and system access credentials.
* Physically seize and secure all unauthorized storage devices found in the suspect's possession.
* Isolate the suspect's workstation from the corporate network to prevent further data movement or remote wiping.
* Implement a temporary block on all USB mass storage device classes via Group Policy Object (GPO) across the affected department.

### Eradication & Recovery
* Perform a full forensic wipe and reimage of the suspect's workstation to remove any remaining anti-forensic tools or artifacts.
* Conduct a comprehensive audit of the suspect's file access logs to identify any other potentially compromised intellectual property.
* Purge all unauthorized LNK files and temporary artifacts identified in the user's profile directory.

### Post-Incident Activity
* Enhance DLP (Data Loss Prevention) policies to trigger real-time alerts for any file copy operations to removable media.
* Integrate endpoint telemetry with a SIEM to monitor for the creation of LNK files pointing to non-standard or removable drive letters.
* Conduct mandatory security awareness training focusing on the risks of insider threats and the strict prohibition of unauthorized hardware.
* Review and tighten access control lists (ACLs) for sensitive project directories to ensure that even managerial roles follow the principle of least privilege.
