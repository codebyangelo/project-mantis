## 6. Executive Incident Synthesis
**Who Attributed:** Internal employee ('Mr. Informant'), a manager in the technology development division, acting in collusion with an external 'Spy Conspirator' from a rival company.

**What Happened:** The suspect exfiltrated sensitive corporate intellectual property, specifically project design concepts and proposals, by copying them onto unauthorized removable media (USB drive) in violation of company security policy.

**Where Transferred:** Removable USB storage device (Drive E:), specifically identified as 'RM#1'.

**How Stolen:** The suspect utilized Windows shortcut (.LNK) files to access and manage sensitive documents stored on an unauthorized external USB drive. The activity was facilitated by the suspect's internal access privileges, which allowed them to bypass standard Data Loss Prevention (DLP) and Digital Rights Management (DRM) controls.

**When Occurred:** 2015-03-23 between 18:37:54Z and 18:38:23Z.

**Incident Narrative:**
On March 23, 2015, the suspect, identified as an internal manager, engaged in unauthorized data exfiltration by transferring sensitive project files to an external USB storage device. Forensic analysis of the 'cfreds_2015_data_leakage_pc' image revealed the presence of malicious LNK files within the user's Recent items folder. These shortcuts specifically pointed to files located on an external drive (E:\RM#1\Secret Project Data\), confirming that the suspect was actively interacting with restricted data stored on non-authorized media. This activity directly violated corporate security policies prohibiting the use of personal or unauthorized storage devices for confidential information. The suspect's actions were part of a broader, premeditated plan to leak proprietary technology to a rival organization for financial gain. The incident was eventually detected when the suspect attempted to remove the storage devices from the company premises, leading to their interception at a security checkpoint.

## 7. NIST SP 800-61r2 Alignment & Containment Recommendations
**Incident Lifecycle Phase:** Detection & Analysis -> Transitioning to Containment, Eradication, & Recovery

### Immediate Containment Actions
* Immediately revoke the suspect's network and system access credentials.
* Physically seize and secure all identified removable media (USB drives, CDs) associated with the suspect.
* Isolate the suspect's workstation from the corporate network to prevent further data synchronization or remote wiping.

### Eradication & Recovery
* Perform a full forensic wipe and reimage of the compromised workstation.
* Conduct a comprehensive audit of all network shares and cloud storage accounts accessed by the suspect to identify the full scope of the data breach.
* Purge all unauthorized LNK files and temporary artifacts identified during the investigation.

### Post-Incident Activity
* Implement stricter hardware-level controls to block unauthorized USB mass storage devices via Group Policy or Endpoint Detection and Response (EDR) solutions.
* Enhance DLP monitoring to alert on the movement of sensitive files to external drives, regardless of user privilege levels.
* Conduct mandatory security awareness training focusing on the risks of insider threats and the importance of adhering to data handling policies.
