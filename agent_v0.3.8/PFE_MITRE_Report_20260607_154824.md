## 6. Executive Incident Synthesis
**Who Attributed:** Mr. Informant (Internal Insider/Manager of Technology Development Division)

**What Happened:** The insider exfiltrated sensitive project files, specifically '[secret_project]_design_concept.ppt' and '[secret_project]_proposal.docx', to an unauthorized external storage device (E:\ drive).

**Where Transferred:** Unauthorized external USB storage device (E:\ drive).

**How Stolen:** The suspect utilized Windows LNK files to facilitate and track access to files stored on an unauthorized external drive, bypassing internal DLP/DRM controls due to their elevated administrative privileges.

**When Occurred:** 2015-03-23 between 18:37:54Z and 18:38:23Z.

**Incident Narrative:**
On March 23, 2015, the suspect, identified as a manager within the technology development division, engaged in unauthorized data exfiltration. Evidence recovered from the workstation indicates that the suspect accessed sensitive project documentation, specifically a design concept and a proposal, and transferred them to an unauthorized external storage device mapped as the E:\ drive. The creation of LNK files in the user's recent items folder confirms that these files were actively accessed from the external media, serving as a primary indicator of the exfiltration event. This activity was part of a broader, premeditated plan by the insider to leak proprietary company data to a rival entity. The suspect leveraged their internal administrative authority to bypass existing Data Loss Prevention (DLP) and Digital Rights Management (DRM) systems, which were otherwise designed to restrict such unauthorized transfers. The incident was eventually identified following security checkpoint detections, leading to the forensic seizure of the suspect's devices.

## 7. NIST SP 800-61r2 Alignment & Containment Recommendations
**Incident Lifecycle Phase:** Detection & Analysis -> Transitioning to Containment, Eradication, & Recovery

### Immediate Containment Actions
* Immediately revoke the suspect's network and system access credentials.
* Physically isolate the suspect's workstation from the corporate network.
* Implement a temporary block on all unauthorized USB and external storage device mounting via Group Policy Object (GPO).
* Identify and disable any active sessions or cloud storage synchronization tokens associated with the suspect's account.

### Eradication & Recovery
* Perform a full forensic wipe and re-image of the compromised workstation.
* Conduct a comprehensive audit of the suspect's file access logs to determine the full scope of data exfiltration.
* Purge all malicious LNK artifacts and temporary files identified during the investigation.
* Reset all administrative passwords and service account credentials that the suspect may have had access to.

### Post-Incident Activity
* Enhance DLP policies to trigger real-time alerts for any file movement to non-corporate external storage devices.
* Implement stricter 'Least Privilege' access controls to ensure managers cannot bypass security protocols.
* Integrate User and Entity Behavior Analytics (UEBA) to detect anomalous file access patterns in real-time.
* Conduct mandatory insider threat awareness training for all employees with access to sensitive technology development data.
